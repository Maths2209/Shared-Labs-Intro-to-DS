Exercise 6

Plot the ROC curve of the model fit from Exercise 4 and compute the corresponding area under the curve (AUC). Do these results support the answer that you give in Exercise 4 to the question What do you think of the usefulness of this model fit to determine credit worthiness? Please explain why or why not.

library(ROCR)
predObj = prediction(data_withprob$PredictedProbability, data_withprob$SeriousDlqin2yrs)
rocObj = performance(predObj, measure="tpr", x.measure="fpr")
aucObj = performance(predObj, measure="auc")
plot(rocObj, main = paste("Area under the curve:",
round(aucObj@y.values[[1]] ,4)))
abline(0,1, col = "red")

We want our model to have an AUC which is close to 1 as that indicates a low false positive rate and a high true positive rate. The model we have however, has an AUC 0.576 which is very far from 1 and closer to 0.5, which would be the AUC for random guessing. This supports our belief that the model is not useful. In fact, the ROC curve we attain is quite similar to the line y=x (in red) which indicates that the models predictions are not much better than predictions made from coin flips.

In linear regression, the coefficients are determined by minimizing the sum of the squared residuals. Give a conceptual description of how the coefficients are determined for the logistic regression fit from the previous exercise.

The coefficients for the logistic regression fit are determined by the maximum likelihood estimation (MLE) method. The MLE method finds the coefficient for each predictor, under which the observed y outcome are most likely. We use the log-likelihood function to determine the coefficient values that maximize it.
