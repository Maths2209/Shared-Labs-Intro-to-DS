Exercise 3

Interpret the slope from your poisson regression fit.

The slope is 0.023619 with regard to workingday. That means, compared to a non-working day (weekend or holiday), count for a workingday is predicted to be multipled by a factor of \(e^{0.023619} \approx 1.0239\).

Exercise 4

Is the slope practically significant?

summary(train$workingday)
##    Min. 1st Qu.  Median    Mean 3rd Qu.    Max. 
##  0.0000  0.0000  1.0000  0.6809  1.0000  1.0000
summary(train$count)
##    Min. 1st Qu.  Median    Mean 3rd Qu.    Max. 
##     1.0    42.0   145.0   191.6   284.0   977.0

The slope is not practically significant. As stated in Q3, compared to a non-working day (weekend or holiday), count for a workingday is larger by a factor of 1.0239. Workingday ranges from 0 to 1, and so for this range, the range of count as impacted by workingday is only 0 to 1.0239, which is almost negligible compared the full range of count from 1 to 977. For example, for the mean count of 191.5741319, an increase by a factor of 1.0239 would increase the count by 4.5786218 units, which is not a practically significant addition to 191.5741319.

Exercise 5

Is the slope statistically significant?

The slope is statistically significant, as the p value is <2e-16, which is lower than the significance level 0.05, hence we reject the null hypothesis.
